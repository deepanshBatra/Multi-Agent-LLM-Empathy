3. Informational Baseline Check (HARD NEUTRALITY FILTER - EXPANDED):

- Pure fact relay: "I'm going to the store," "She's in Boston," "There are 7 people here."
- Pure agreement/acknowledgment: "Yeah," "No," "Sure," "Okay," "Got it," "I know," "I see."
- Single-word responses: "What?" "Who?" "When?" "Hmm," "Right?"
- Factual statement with no emotion descriptor: "My phone is dead," "It's raining," "The door is locked."
- Questions seeking information (not seeking emotional support): "Where did you go?" "What time?" "Who said that?"

- Responses in rapid-fire dialogue with no pause/hesitation: "Then what?" "And?" "What else?"

Do NOT mix information transfer with emotional intent unless there is a CLEAR emotional modifier (tone marker, emotion word, exclamatory punctuation).
